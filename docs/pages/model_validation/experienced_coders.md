# Instructions: Experienced Coders

### Activity: Interpreting Climate Data

Complete the tutorial independently. Then, with your partner, complete the following exercise:

1. Present each of your figures to your partner. This will be a pretty informal presentation, but think about telling an overall story of areas in which the model is reliable and areas in which it has notable biases. During the presentation, the other person should ask questions and give feedback. The presenter can also ask questions and solicit feedback if there are things they are unsure about! Then switch roles, and have the other person present.
2. When both people have had the chance to present, brainstorm together some next steps you could take; how could you further investigate any biases you found? What additional analyses might help you assess the model and its limitations? These next steps could be the same or different for the two partners.
3. Work independently to implement one or two of these ideas and create a couple new figures.
4. Come back together to do another round of presentations.

I am happy to help you troubleshoot code, interpret figures, and brainstorm ideas for next steps; just raise a hand, and I will come over! I will also give you some guidance on when it is time to move between these steps, but feel free to work at a pace that works for your pair.
