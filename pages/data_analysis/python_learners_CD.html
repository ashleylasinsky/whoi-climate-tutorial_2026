Instructions: Python Learners#

Pair Programming Activity#

You will be working together to complete this tutorial in pairs, sharing one computer and taking turns in the roles of “pilot” and “navigator.” The pilot is responsible for typing the code, and explaining aloud each step of their process. The navigator is responsible for asking questions, making suggestions if the pilot needs help or if the navigator has an idea for something to try, and looking out for errors. These roles are meant as a guideline; the most important thing is that you are working together! However, it is critical that only one person is typing at a time. Switch roles between each of the following steps in the tutorial:

  1. Import necessary packages and load the dataset. Plot a sample from the data.

  2. Define and compute an index

  3. Make two plots showing your index: a spatial plot and a time series

  4. Compute the seasonal cycle, anomalies, and a linear trend. Plot these.

  5. Correlate your index with some spatial data. Plot this.

  6. Create a histogram and a power spectrum from your index.

  7. If you have more time: play around with it! What other ways might you want to manipulate or visualize the data? Keep switching roles every few minutes

This pair programming thing might seem a little strange at first! Why are we having you do this? The goal of this exercise is for you to not just copy-paste the example code provided in the tutorial, but be thinking actively about how it works and why you are making these choices. One great way to do that is to talk through it with a peer! Especially with AI coding tools, memorizing syntax isn’t as important, while understanding how code works and how to interpret it scientifically is more important than ever. You could do this tutorial on your own any time; we want to make use of this in-class time as an opportunity for you to get to know your peers and gain the deeper understanding that comes with collaboration. Also, we hope it will be more fun than just sitting alone staring at your computer screen!

I will also be circulating the room, so please raise a hand if your pair needs help!

At the end of class, make sure you send the finished script to your partner.
